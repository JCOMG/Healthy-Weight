What is BMR?

Basal metabolic rate (BMR) refers to the minimum energy expenditure required by a person to maintain life activities in a resting state without eating and when the ambient temperature is suitable. This includes energy requirements to maintain breathing, heartbeat, body temperature, and other basic physiological functions. Example: Assuming a 30-year-old man weighs 70 kilograms and is 175 centimeters tall, his BMR may be about 1685 kcal/day. This means that even if he does nothing all day long, his body will consume these calories to maintain basic life activities.

What is RMR?
Resting metabolic rate (RMR) is similar to basal metabolic rate, but the measurement conditions are more relaxed and usually refers to the metabolic rate in a non-sleeping, relaxed state. It is slightly higher than the basal metabolic rate because it includes the energy required for light activities such as sitting, standing, small movements, etc. Example: Using the same 30-year-old male, if he sat most of the time and engaged in light activity, his RMR would probably be about 1900 kcal/day. This means that his body will burn these calories throughout the day even if he is not doing any strenuous activity.
